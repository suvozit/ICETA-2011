Art of Defense

Recent advancements in mobile processors (CPUs and GPUs) have enabled researchers to explore 3D augmented reality applications on handheld devices. Art of Defense (AoD) is a novel AR game for a commodity phone (the Nokia N95). The goal of Art Of Defense is to explore game design and interaction techniques that are well suited to mobile AR on a small screen device. One principle guiding AoD is to uses the phones limitations as part of the game itself; in this case, designing assuming the player has a limited view of a larger game space.

Super Quasi Black Hole

This was my final project for the optimization class taught by Dr. Karen Liu. I wrote a LCP solver while Kane implemented a Quasi-Newton method. I used his solver to find 2D projectile trajectories in arbitrary gravitational fields defined by the user such that it would reach the target in a given number of steps by picking the correct angle and velocity.

Links: Main project page

SLAR: An Augmented Reality interface to Second Life

This project was originally developed by Tobias Lang at Georgia Institute of Technology in 2007. It allows one to essentially blend real physical spaces with virtual places in Second Life. Brian Davidson and I maintained and extended this project in 2008 to handheld devices using streamed video.

Instant Radiosity Rendering for Augmented Environments

Along with Cristobal Alvarez, I implemented a version of Keller's Instant Radiosity algorithm and tested it in an augmented environment that is viewed using a head mounted display. This was the final project for CS7491: Advanced Image Synthesis under Dr. Greg Turk.

Bomberman Mod for UT2004

The Bomberman mod is a tribute to the old Sega game by the same name, and tries to involve most elements of the original. The aim is to trap opponents by placing bombs in strategic locations on the map. Players gain points by wiping out opponents and destroying obstacles. The player with the highest score at the end of the playing time wins the round. Weapons include a normal bomb that detonates after a fixed period of time as well as a remote control that allows the player to place a number of bombs and detonate them all at once. The player can use the map to detect the positions of opponents as well as bombs.

I was part of a six member team and my responsibilities involved game design, object modeling, and scripting of general gameplay, weapons and network components.

Wake of Destruction

An arcade style game based on physics. Break and burn everything you can in a room before it collapses under its own weight. Developed using the Newton physics SDK and the C4 game engine. I was the lead programmer and worked with Chris Occhipinti (artwork, design) and Luis Cruz (video, UI).
